Section 02
Beginning of Day
BOD · Before Huddle
The 5-Step BOD Checklist
~30 min
Everything in this list happens before the morning sales huddle. If you walk into the huddle without knowing your overnight numbers and hot leads, you're already behind. Coffee in hand. Slack open. Let's go.
01
Check your calendar. Pull up today's triage calls. Note prospect names, IG handles, and what bucket they fall into (Goal / Problem / PT Pivot).
02
Check the CRMs for overnight leads. Both HubSpot (opt-ins from VSL, ads, masterclass) and GHL (DM-generated leads). Anything that came in after 9pm yesterday gets priority.
03
Check Slack. Scan team channels for relevant updates — campaign launches, hot leads tagged by other setters or Taeler, anything that changes your day's plan.
04
Score and post overnight bookings. Drop call notes in #02-calls-booked with the 1️⃣–4️⃣ lead score (see scoring system below).
05
Post your BOD report in #sales-team Slack. Use the template below. Don't freelance the format — managers read these fast.
BOD Report Template — Paste into #sales-team
BOD [DATE] # of new leads in GHL/HubSpot overnight: # of triage calls on the calendar: - NAME OF LEAD // @handle - NAME OF LEAD // @handle Hot leads I'll make offers to today: - NAME OF LEAD // @handle Goals for the Day: - Goal 1 - Goal 2 - Goal 3
BOD · Lead Scoring
The 1–4 Lead Score System
~5 min/lead
Every booking you score goes in #02-calls-booked with a score emoji. This is how the closer prioritizes prep time — a 4️⃣ gets full pre-call research, a 1️⃣ gets removed. Be honest. Inflating a score helps no one and tanks your live close rate.
1️⃣
Score 1
Remove
Robot, unqualified, joke booking. Pull from calendar.
2️⃣
Score 2
Approve (Caveat)
Qualified but financially weak or low-rapport. Approve unless from ads.
3️⃣
Score 3
Good Lead
Solid prospect. Confident the closer can close.
4️⃣
Score 4
Our People
Fantastic lead. 100% ready to buy. Full closer prep.
Section 03
Mid-Day Operations
Always-On Rules
Inbox & DM Management — Non-Negotiable Standards
10m
Speed-to-lead. Respond to every new lead and unread DM within 10 minutes during business hours. Decay curve is steep.
2T
Two tabs. Open every active DM conversation in a second tab so nothing slips through the cracks.
📝
Notes after every exchange. Update the GHL/HubSpot contact card. Pristine data = paid commissions.
5x
Five-touch minimum. Follow up with leads 5 times before deactivating. 74% don't engage until touch 5.

Mid-Day · Dial Blocks
Outbound Dial Sessions
60–90 min focus windows
Dial sessions get blocked off so you're not bouncing between DMs and phone every 5 minutes. Phone work and DM work require different brains — protect both by batching. Target: 80–100 dials/day across your dial blocks. The real metric is qualified conversations, not total dials.
01
Pull your dial queue from Aloware. Prioritize: new VSL/lead magnet opt-ins → Warm List no-shows → Hot List 5-day-no-contacts.
02
Mute your DMs. Phone session means phone session. DM responses can wait 60 minutes.
03
Use the "Hey, [name] just—" pickup hack. Cut yourself off mid-sentence. Implied familiarity + curiosity loop = dramatically higher pickup rates. (See Outbound Dialing Script.)
04
Update CRM notes after every call. Doesn't matter if it was a voicemail, hang-up, or 20-minute conversation — log it before the next dial.
05
SMS fallback for no-answers. Text within 5 min: "Hey [Name] — [Setter Name] from IFCA. Tried you just now re: the [VSL/lead magnet]. When's a good 5 min today or tomorrow?"
Mid-Day · DM Sessions
Inbound DMs & IG Sourcing
Throughout day · Block sourcing 2x
Inbound DM responses happen throughout the day at the 10-minute SLA. Sourcing (cold outbound DMs to new followers, story viewers, post engagers) happens in two dedicated 45-minute blocks — usually morning and afternoon. Target: 50–75 new IG conversations started per day.
01
Inbound first. Anyone who DMs a keyword, replies to a story, or comments on a post gets a response within 10 minutes. Use the 4-step DM script.
02
Source from new followers daily. Both IFCA and Taeler's accounts. Profile-personalized opener — never copy/paste.
03
Hit story viewers and engaged commenters. Story viewers especially — they're warm. Open with reference to the content they engaged with.
04
15–30 meaningful comments/reactions on prospect content. Warms the IG algorithm and builds rapport before the DM lands.
05
Move every contacted lead to ACTIVE in the 01-SETTER pipeline. Assign the lead to yourself in GHL. Untracked = unpaid.
Section 04
End of Day
EOD · Daily Wrap
The 8-Step EOD Checklist
~45–60 min
EOD is where tomorrow gets won. Confirming tomorrow's calls is the single highest-leverage show-rate lever you have — skip it and your show rate drops by 15–20%. Do these in order. Don't shortcut step 7.
01
Check calendar for tomorrow's triage calls. Note prospect names, handles, buckets, and any pre-call prep needed.
02
Final CRM sweep. Last-minute new leads in HubSpot/GHL. Anything that comes in after 5pm gets a response or gets queued for tomorrow's BOD.
03
Final Slack sweep. Any hot lead alerts, tag-team requests from Taeler or other setters, or campaign updates.
04
Update call notes for new bookings. Post in #02-calls-booked + update the CRM contact card. Two places, same notes.
05
Fill out the EOD form in Airtable. Detailed notes on hot leads, warm leads, and anyone currently warming up.
06
Fill out the FCA Setter Tracker spreadsheet. Daily numbers — dials, DMs started, sets, shows, qualified calls.
07
⚠ Confirm tomorrow's appointments. Personal text/DM to every booked lead. "Hey [Name], just confirming our call tomorrow at [time] — looking forward to it!" This is the show-rate save. Do not skip.
08
Post EOD in #sales-team. Use the template below. Sets your team up for the morning huddle.
EOD Report Template — Paste into #sales-team
EOD [DATE] Hot Leads to Watch: - NAME OF LEAD // @handle - NAME OF LEAD // @handle Reminders/Follow-Ups: - Notes for team on active follow-ups, tech issues, etc. Notes for leads "warming up," "warm," or "hot": - NAME OF LEAD // @handle — notes - NAME OF LEAD // @handle — notes Tomorrow's Confirmed Calls: [count]

⚡ The Single Highest-Leverage Metric
Set-to-show is the most coachable number in this role.
You can't directly control whether the closer closes — that depends on the closer's skill and the prospect's readiness. But you own whether the prospect shows up live. Confirmation cadence, pre-call priming, reschedule handling, no-show recovery — these are the levers. The difference between 50% and 70% set-to-show is the difference between $58K and $91K in annual comp. Master these levers first.
