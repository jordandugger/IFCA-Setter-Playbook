Feedback Framework
The COIN Method
How we give and receive feedback at IFCA. Replaces the older PFFF model. Feedback should be safe, consistent, and usable — fact-based but human, not scripted. Use this whenever you're coaching a teammate, debriefing a call, or receiving notes from a manager.
C
Context
The specific situation or setting where the behavior occurred.
O
Observation
The factual action or behavior you noticed — what happened, not how you felt.
I
Impact
The result or effect of that action on the outcome, team, or prospect.
N
Next
The encouragement to repeat or the adjustment to make going forward.
Delivery Rules
Start with consent
Keep it short and real
Use facts not feelings
No need to tie to core values

The Three Buckets
Who We Sell To
Every prospect you talk to falls into one of three buckets — Goal Syntax, Problem Syntax, or PT Pivot. The closer runs a different script for each. Your job is to pattern-match the bucket within the first two minutes so you can pre-frame the call correctly and the closer walks in ready. Use the recognition signals below to identify the bucket from a profile, a DM, or the first 60 seconds of a dial.
Bucket 01 · Goal Syntax
Starting From Scratch
< $2K/month
The aspiring or just-starting coach. No established online business yet — they're in a W-2 job, just left one, or making inconsistent side-income under $2K/month. The problem isn't a broken business. The problem is the absence of one. Fear, doubt, and uncertainty are the dominant emotions. They want a different life and don't know how to get there.
✓ Recognition Signals (Bio + Profile)
  • Bio mentions "future coach," "aspiring," "soon-to-be," or a current job title (nurse, teacher, gym staff)
  • Sub-1K followers OR low engagement on a larger account
  • Posts about their own transformation, not client transformations
  • No clear offer, price, or "work with me" link in bio
  • Personal account, often no business handle yet
✓ Recognition Signals (What They Say)
  • "I want to start coaching online"
  • "I'm a [PT / nurse / trainer] and I want to build something on the side"
  • "I want to leave my 9-to-5"
  • "I don't know where to start"
  • "I have no clients yet" or "I just got my first one"
What they actually mean
"I want freedom, I'm scared, I don't believe I can pull this off, and I need someone to show me the exact steps before I waste two more years figuring it out alone."

Bucket 02 · Problem Syntax
Established With a Constraint
$2K – $40K+/month
The coach with a real online business — but something specific is broken or capped. They know the problem (inconsistent leads, low close rate, churning clients, agency burn, plateau at $8-15K/mo). The closer's job is to pinpoint the constraint, quantify the gap, and position IFCA as the precise fix. They don't need motivation. They need a diagnosis.
✓ Recognition Signals (Bio + Profile)
  • Bio names a niche + outcome ("Helping busy moms lose 20 lbs," "$10K/month online coach")
  • Clear offer or "work with me" link in bio
  • Posts feature client testimonials, transformations, social proof
  • 1K-25K followers with real engagement
  • Business handle, branded grid, content schedule visible
✓ Recognition Signals (What They Say)
  • "I'm doing $8K/month and I'm stuck"
  • "My leads aren't converting" or "My close rate is low"
  • "I got burned by an agency" / "I tried Facebook ads and lost $X"
  • "I have clients but I'm trading time for money"
  • "My back-end is broken" / "Clients churn after 3 months"
What they actually mean
"I built something that works but I've hit a wall I can't see past. I need someone who can look at my numbers and tell me exactly what's broken — and prove they can fix it."

Bucket 03 · PT Pivot
In-Person → Hybrid / Online
In-person revenue · Time-capped
The credentialed in-person personal trainer who wants out of the gym floor. They're capped by the physical and time ceiling — can't see another client past 5pm, body's wrecked, family time is gone. The closer leads with the Three Currencies: money, body, family. Not just the income gap. They have a real skill and a real client base — they need the system to translate it online.
✓ Recognition Signals (Bio + Profile)
  • Bio mentions "PT," "personal trainer," "S&C coach," gym name, or in-person studio
  • Posts feature in-person client training, gym backdrops, workout demos
  • Local geographic focus (city, region, specific gym)
  • Credentials displayed (CSCS, CPT, NASM, etc.)
  • Often a smaller follower count (200-3K) but loyal local audience
✓ Recognition Signals (What They Say)
  • "I want to take my business online" / "I want to go hybrid"
  • "I'm burnt out doing 6am to 8pm sessions"
  • "My body can't keep doing this forever"
  • "I have a waitlist and can't take more clients"
  • "I want to scale beyond my hours"
What they actually mean
"My body is breaking down, I'm missing my kids growing up, and the only way out is to figure out how to deliver this skill online — but I don't know how, and I'm scared I'll lose the relationships I built in-person."

The Filter
Who We Don't Sell To
⚠ Disqualifiers
If they fit any of these, do not set the call.
Setting a call with someone outside our ICP wastes the closer's time, drops your close rate, hurts your commission, and burns goodwill. A bad set is worse than no set. Use this list as your hard filter — if they hit even one, politely disqualify and move on.
  • Not a fitness/health/wellness coach — we only serve this niche
  • Looking to launch a non-coaching business — e-com, real estate, agency
  • Cannot invest $5K minimum — under credit/cash floor
  • "Just shopping for info" — no real intent to act
  • Already in a competing program — currently enrolled
  • Hostile or red-flag energy in DMs — abusive, harassing, or unstable
  • Asking only about money / get-rich-quick — no coaching commitment
  • Outside our target geographies — if non-English speaker without translation context
