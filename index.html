The Setter Creed
Our only job as a setter
"We don't sell the program.
We sell the appointment."
01
We sell the appointment — never the program. If we find ourselves typing a paragraph explaining IFCA, we've already lost the prospect. The closer pitches the offer. We surface pain and book the call.
02
We protect the closer's calendar. Every call we set is a real human with surfaced pain, financial qualification, and the intent to show up. A bad set is worse than no set — it burns the closer's time and our own commission.
03
We own the show rate. The lead showed up because we confirmed at 24 hours, primed at one hour, and re-engaged when they ghosted — not because we got lucky. The set is half the job. The show is the other half.
The fundamental temptation: Prospects ask "what is this?" and your instinct is to explain. Don't. The setter who explains the offer is the setter who loses the call. Diagnose first. Sell the appointment. The closer pitches the program.
